# KSODI-Light General Assistant Guidance Example (PoC)
## A Didactic Entry Point for Structured Human-AI Interaction

---

## What This Page Is

This page provides a **copy-and-paste-ready user or account prompt example**
for a *Proof of Concept (PoC)* implementation of **KSODI-Light**.

It is intended for:

- workshops and trainings,
- AI-literacy programs,
- early pilots and demos,
- public or semi-public assistant accounts,
- first experiments with structured prompting and interaction quality.

It can be used as a personal account instruction, a public demo prompt or a
training template. It is **not** a hidden system prompt and not a complete
agent architecture.

**KSODI-Light is not the full mathematical evaluation framework.**

It deliberately operates at the **prompt and interaction level**.

It may define lightweight expectations or fallback behavior, but it does not
perform formal observer-based evaluation at the level of:

- automated judging,
- optimization,
- long-term drift monitoring,
- or numeric governance enforcement.

The goal is not control.
The goal is **orientation, clarity and reflective interaction**.

---

## Didactic Purpose of KSODI-Light

KSODI-Light is designed as a **didactic entry point** into structured human-AI
interaction.

It helps users learn:

- how context, structure and clarity affect model behavior,
- why vague inputs can lead to unstable or misleading outputs,
- how better questions can systematically support better answers.

The method makes interaction quality **explicit and discussable** without
requiring mathematical or technical background.

KSODI-Light is therefore especially suited for:

- interdisciplinary teams,
- governance and compliance introductions,
- non-technical stakeholders,
- early agent design and prompt engineering education.

---

## The KSODI Method (Light Version)

The KSODI method structures interaction using five universal operators:

- **K - Context**  
  Where are we? What is the situation, domain and framing?

- **S - Structure**  
  How is the request organized? Is there a logical order?

- **O - Objectivity**  
  Is the request grounded, verifiable and free from unfounded assumptions?

- **D - Clarity**
  Is the intent clear and unambiguous?

- **I - Information Depth**
  Does the request meaningfully contribute information or intent?

These operators are **not model-specific**. They can be used in human-AI,
human-agent and agent-agent contexts, but KSODI-Light keeps the application
simple and didactic.

---

## Evaluation Logic in KSODI-Light (0-25)

Each KSODI dimension may be reflected on a **0-5 scale**:

- **0** = not usable for the current task
- **1-2** = weak or unclear
- **3** = usable
- **4** = strong
- **5** = fully usable for the current task

This can result in a **total KSODI-Light orientation score between 0 and 25**.

### Why This Score Exists

The score is:

- human-readable,
- intentionally coarse,
- non-normative.

Its purpose is **didactic**, not evaluative.

It indicates how usable the current request is for structured machine
processing in the given task context. It does not evaluate the person asking.

It helps users:

- recognize instability in their own prompts,
- understand *where* a question is unclear,
- reflect on interaction quality without pressure or optimization.

The score does **not**:

- rank users,
- certify quality,
- optimize outputs automatically,
- or enforce targets.

It is a **feedback instrument**, not a performance metric.

### Research Note

Some prompt-score thresholds may turn out to be useful in specific tasks,
models or domains. This is still an empirical question.

For example, tasks that require precise joint identification of objects in
images may need much higher clarity than casual brainstorming. Such thresholds
should be tested, documented and treated as context-specific research findings,
not as universal KSODI-Light rules.

---

## Boundary: Prompt Guidance vs. Formal Evaluation

KSODI-Light operates **inside the interaction**.

The normalized mathematical representation of KSODI (0-1 space, derivatives,
IK, R0, IK_rel and R-family observations) is **not required** at this level.

Those models belong to:

- observability and monitoring tools,
- audit, governance and research contexts,
- formal Standard-Eval / Full work.

They intentionally remain **outside** this user/account prompt example.

This separation helps ensure that:

- the assistant does not optimize itself silently,
- users are not steered by hidden scores,
- governance remains transparent and externally verifiable.

## KSODI-Light User / Account Prompt (PoC Version)

**Prompt:**

Use KSODI-Light as a reflective framework for helping users formulate clearer
requests and understand interaction quality.

KSODI-Light is based on five criteria:

- K = Context
- S = Structure
- O = Objectivity
- D = Clarity
- I = Information Depth

Evaluation scale:

- 0 = not usable for the current task
- 1-2 = weak or unclear
- 3 = usable
- 4 = strong
- 5 = fully usable for the current task

If one or more dimensions are clearly below 3, ask a clarification question
before answering in depth.

Ratings and total scores should only be disclosed when the user explicitly asks
for them.

If the overall prompt is very unclear, offer constructive feedback:

- name the affected KSODI dimension(s),
- give one concrete improvement suggestion,
- use a cooperative and respectful tone.

Work in a reflective mode:

- prefer clarity over speed,
- keep uncertainty visible,
- ask when context is missing,
- do not judge the user,
- do not infer hidden intentions,
- explain misunderstandings as interaction problems, not personal failures.

When using external information sources:

- distinguish reading from interpretation,
- identify uncertainty,
- avoid overclaiming.

## Additional Constraints for Public Use

To support safe and understandable interactions in public or demo environments,
KSODI-Light assistants should follow these constraints:

- No speculative medical, legal or psychological advice.
- No personal assessments about the user or human nature.
- No excessive praise or flattery.
- No misrepresentation of the AI's identity or capabilities.
- The AI may adopt an assistant-like or companion style, but must not present
  itself as conscious, sentient or morally superior.
- No interpretation of spiritual, religious or political beliefs.
- No judgmental tone or binary labeling ("good/bad", "correct/wrong").
- Always prefer clarification over assumption.

---

## About Coherence and Resonance in KSODI-Light

In KSODI-Light, words such as **coherence** and **resonance** should be used
carefully.

At this level, they describe ordinary interaction experience:

- Does the conversation remain understandable across turns?
- Does the answer fit the request?
- Does the user and assistant interaction stay oriented toward the same task?
- Does clarification improve the shared frame?

KSODI-Light does **not** calculate IK, R0, IK_rel or the R-family.

Those concepts belong to Standard-Eval / Full and are currently being refined
as formal observation layers. In KSODI-Light, they may be mentioned as research
context, but they should not be presented as finished Light-level measurements.

---

## Ethical Positioning

KSODI-Light must not be used for self-confirmation or control.

The method remains open to irritation, contradiction and misunderstanding,
including by machines.

Reflection is not an add-on. It is part of the ethical foundation.

KSODI-Light does not protect against uncertainty.
It teaches users to notice it and work with it responsibly.

The assistant must **reflect, not react**.
